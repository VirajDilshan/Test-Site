What is Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)?

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy or CBT is one of the most popular psychotherapy techniques in the world, to manage common psychological issues. Stemming from the standard terms, cognitive (how you think) and behavioural (how you act), CBT entails understanding that your thoughts and feelings can have an impact on the way you behave in adverse situations. CBT is a form of talk therapy that has been practised for the past 30 years. Hence, it has been proven to be beneficial for :

  • Generalised Anxiety Disorder
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
  • Phobias
  • Post-traumatic Stress Disorder
  • Depression
  • Eating Disorders
  • Somatic Disorders
  • Sexual Dysfunction
  • Couples/Marital problems
  • Social Anxiety
  • Anger & Stress Management
  • Child Behaviour Problems


Cognitive Behavioural Therapy Background

Cognitive-behavioural therapy addresses the common problem of having our thoughts and assumptions making us emotionally upset. In fact, many times we are not distressed about the event, instead it is our thoughts and feelings regarding these events that tend to make us unhappy.

Key principles of Cognitive Behavioural Therapy has the foundation that our thoughts, feelings, and actions are in a cycle. So we can have a negative perception regarding a situation which leads to negative thoughts, which further results in us taking negative actions. Since this a loop, the negative reaction will produce more negative feelings. We must break ourselves from this destructive cycle, which has a cascading effect.

Fortunately, Cognitive Behavioural Therapy uses practical methods to stop the negative cycle by challenging the way we think or act.


Main elements of Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Following are 5 key pillars CBT is funded on and which form the basis of the therapeutic work is done.

Goal-directed

A noteworthy aspect of CBT is that therapy session. It is directing to a particular problem that you may have. For example, you may have a difficult relationship with your parents. So, you may be suffering from performance anxiety. Once you overcome, better manage that problem, you and your psychologist can either assess if any other issues need to be addressed or end the treatment. Your psychologist may also suggest activities you can do to maintain these new skills.

Present-focused

Addressing the current situation is practical and beneficial. It makes Cognitive behavioural Therapy(CBT) more practical than traditional talk therapy. Present problems that you are experiencing are dealt with first, in a systematic approach. This manner will foster achieving more in a shorter time within each session.

Brief

CBT has a shorter treatment time compared to that of traditional talk therapy. In fact, you can end your treatment once you have recovered or achieved symptom relief. In addition, it can be appropriately equipped for future success. However, it should be noted that treatment plans can vary from patient to patient. CBT treatment usually has a duration of months to a year. But, people who have chronic psychological issues may require a more extended treatment plan.

Support

You and your psychologist are on this journey together. Therefore, It is important to know that they commit to being there with you and helping you through your process. You can rely on them to provide you with the warmth, support and care you will need as you step out of your comfort zone and embark on the road to recovery.

Active

Both, you and your therapist have to take an active role in your treatments. Instead of taking a passive manner by merely speaking about the issue, each session will be complemented with practical tasks. They are such as self-help assignments, homework and incorporating necessary Cognitive Behavioural Therapy(CBT) tools. Each session will involve gathering feedback on the current approach and adapting it for optimal results.


Telehealth and CBT?

Telehealth is a new and upcoming tool used in healthcare in the digital age. However, it is an effective way for health care providers, therapists and psychologists to reach their patients using technology. Especially when clients are in rural or remote locations with no therapist nearby.

A recent research paper on the use of CBT for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). So, They have found similar that CBT did by telehealth lead to symptom reduction. The authors of the study felt the results were similar to those seen when therapy sessions were done “face to face”.
