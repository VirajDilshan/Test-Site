Reasons to choose an online psychiatrist

A psychiatrist can provide you with a wide variety of psychiatric services to treat mental health problems. Such as depression, anxiety, obsessive-compulsive disorder or stress. There are a number of reasons a person may consider seeing an online psychiatrist instead of visiting one in person. For example;

  • Accessing health care online, including mental health care becoming more normalised. Millions of Australians did their first telehealth appointment in 2020 during the COVID19 pandemic.
  • More and more psychiatrists are transitioning to online practice to meet the increasing demand for telepsychiatry.
  • Telepsychiatry is the best solution to provide care for people over a large area and, in many rural and remote areas, it is easier to access psychiatric services via telehealth than it is to see a psychiatrist face to face.
  • Technology is increasingly becoming a part of our daily lives and Australians are becoming increasingly comfortable with online healthcare options.
  • Rural and underserviced areas do not have many psychiatric clinics. Medicare and the Australian Government have been supporting telehealth in these areas long before COVID19. In fact, psychiatrists have been providing telehealth services to regional Australia for decades.


Benefits of telepsychiatry

While some people may be reluctant or feel awkward talking to a person on a screen, research shows most people are comfortable with it. Some people may be more relaxed and willing to open up from the comfort of their home or a convenient local facility. Also, this will likely be less of a problem as people become more familiar and comfortable with video communication in everyday life

Telepsychiatry helps meet patients’ needs for convenient, affordable and readily accessible mental health services.

It can benefit patients in a number of ways by;

  • Improving access to mental health speciality care that might not otherwise be available (e.g., in rural areas)
  • Bringing care to the patient’s location, so that patients can have the appointments from the comfort of their home
  • Helping to integrate mental health care and GP care, leading to better health outcomes
  • Reducing the need for trips to the emergency room. Patients can request to be seen by a clinician online or after hours from their home
  • Reducing wait time, as telepsychiatrist waitlists tend to be shorter, and patients can request to have the telehealth sessions weekly if necessary
  • Providing more convenient and flexible appointment times for patients
  • Allowing for better continuity of care and consistent follow up appointments
  • Reducing the need for time off work, childcare services, travel time etc. to access appointments
  • Reducing transportation barriers, such as lack of transportation or the need for long drives. In 2017, telehealth saved WA country patients from travelling 27.3 million kilometres for outpatient appointments

Psychiatrists near me for telehealth

There is substantial evidence to support the effectiveness of telepsychiatry and research has found satisfaction to be high among patients, psychiatrists and other health professionals. Telepsychiatry is equivalent to in-person care in diagnostic accuracy, treatment effectiveness, quality of care and patient satisfaction. Patient privacy and confidentiality meet the same standards as in-person care.

With telepsychiatry, patients can schedule appointments individually with a psychiatrist or therapist via live video. Although not essential, it is helpful for patients to prepare for an initial telepsychiatry appointment by having any relevant records and information, including current and prior medications and hospital admissions, on hand. Some patients also benefit from bringing along a list of pre-prepared questions to be addressed in the session.

Given the high-quality care available with telepsychiatry, people no longer need to search for a “psychiatrist near me”.
